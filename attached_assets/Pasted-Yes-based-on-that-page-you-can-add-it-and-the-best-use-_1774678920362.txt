Yes — based on that page, you can add it, and the **best use is as a structure/setup layer**, not as a full replacement for order flow. The site describes the SK System as a **price-action and market-structure method** that tries to identify “sequences” and trade at the **end of corrective movements** to keep stops tight and improve reward/risk. It also says the setup is known **before** the trade and is less dependent on short-term fluctuations. ([SK Trading System][1])

That means SK and order flow solve different problems:

* **SK-style logic** helps answer: **Where should I be interested?**
* **Order flow** helps answer: **Is the market actually confirming right now?**

So for Godsview, the stronger architecture is:

**SK structure filter → setup candidate → order-flow confirmation → ML/Claude/risk**

That fits the page well because the system emphasizes fixed rules, predefined setups, intraday use, and price-action structure rather than indicator-heavy signal chasing. ([SK Trading System][1])

## Will it give “better accuracy” than order flow?

Not automatically.

The page itself does **not** claim high hit rate as the main edge. In fact, it says the **risk/reward ratio is above average, so even a low hit rate could still be profitable**. That is important: this sounds more like a **location + R:R system** than a “super high win-rate” system. ([SK Trading System][1])

So the realistic answer is:

* **SK alone** may improve **trade location**
* **Order flow alone** may improve **timing/confirmation**
* **SK + order flow** is the combination most likely to improve real trade quality

## Best way to add it to Godsview

Build an **SK Engine** that outputs structure features like:

* higher-timeframe bias
* sequence direction
* corrective move in progress / complete
* candidate zone price
* distance to SK zone
* structure alignment score
* expected invalidation level
* R:R potential from current price

Then change your current logic from:

`setup detected anywhere + order flow`

to:

`setup detected only near valid SK structure + order flow confirms`

That is the part most likely to improve accuracy.

## How it should affect your existing setups

### Sweep reclaim

Only allow it when the sweep happens **into an SK-defined structural area** or at the end of a corrective sequence, not in random midrange price. This matches the site’s idea of identifying precise areas from market structure. ([SK Trading System][1])

### Absorption reversal

Only allow it when absorption happens **at the SK zone**, because price-action structure gives the context and order flow gives the trigger. ([SK Trading System][1])

### Continuation

Use SK to define whether the pullback is a valid **corrective move ending within trend structure**, then use order flow to confirm continuation. The site explicitly talks about entering at the conclusion of corrective movements. ([SK Trading System][1])

### Breakout failure

Only take it when the failed break occurs at a structurally meaningful SK area, not just any local high/low.

## What not to do

Do not remove order flow immediately and assume SK is superior. The site is a vendor/product page with educational and marketing framing; it gives the broad philosophy, but it does not provide the kind of independent evidence you would need to conclude that it outperforms order flow across instruments and sessions. ([SK Trading System][1])

## Best implementation plan

Phase it like this:

### Phase 1

Add SK as a **pre-filter**

* mark valid structure zones
* mark corrective completion
* allow setups only near those zones

### Phase 2

Add SK features into your model

* `sk_bias`
* `sk_zone_distance`
* `sk_sequence_score`
* `sk_correction_complete`
* `sk_rr_quality`

### Phase 3

Run ablations
Compare:

* order flow only
* SK only
* SK + order flow

Judge by:

* win rate
* expectancy
* profit factor
* drawdown
* post-cost results
